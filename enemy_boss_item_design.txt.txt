ENEMY AND BOSS DESIGN - Vertical Endless Archer RPG

1) Enemy Design Rules
- Enemies must be easy to read in fast combat.
- Strong silhouette is more important than detail.
- Each enemy type should have one clear role.
- Keep animations short: idle, move, attack, hit, die.
- Use color accents to show danger or elite status.

2) Normal Enemy Types
A. Slime Scout
- Small, simple, fast enemy.
- Color: green or blue.
- Role: low HP, moves quickly.
- Behavior: rushes the player in groups.

B. Goblin Runner
- Small humanoid enemy with weapon.
- Color: brown, green, dark red.
- Role: fast attacker.
- Behavior: zig-zag movement or quick rush.

C. Bat Swarm
- Flying enemy with small wings.
- Color: black or purple.
- Role: annoying swarm unit.
- Behavior: comes in clusters.

D. Skeleton Soldier
- Basic medium enemy.
- Color: bone white and gray.
- Role: balanced unit.
- Behavior: moves steadily, slightly tougher.

E. Wolf Beast
- Fast animal enemy.
- Color: gray, dark brown, red eyes.
- Role: speed pressure.
- Behavior: fast direct charge.

3) Tank Enemy Types
A. Armored Brute
- Large body, heavy armor.
- Color: dark steel, red accents.
- Role: absorbs damage.
- Behavior: slow but dangerous.

B. Shield Skeleton
- Skeleton with big shield.
- Color: bone white, silver, blue glow.
- Role: blocks frontal attacks.
- Behavior: advances slowly.

C. Stone Golem
- Rock body with glowing core.
- Color: gray stone, orange core.
- Role: high HP boss-like unit.
- Behavior: slow movement, heavy attacks.

4) Elite Enemy Types
- Elite enemies are upgraded versions of normal monsters.
- Add glow, aura, extra armor, or stronger color.
- Give them more HP, speed, or special attack.
- Use them to make waves feel less repetitive.

Example elite versions:
- Elite Slime: faster and splits on death.
- Elite Goblin: throws projectiles.
- Elite Skeleton: stronger weapon and more armor.
- Elite Wolf: leaps farther and hits harder.

5) Boss Design Rules
- Boss must be visually larger than normal enemies.
- Boss must have 2 to 4 attack patterns.
- Boss should have phase changes when HP drops.
- Boss should use stronger colors and more effects.
- Boss should feel like a major event every 10 waves.

6) Boss Concepts
A. Corrupted Forest Guardian
- Giant plant and stone guardian.
- Color: dark green, black, glowing purple.
- Attacks: root slam, vine whip, poison wave.
- Theme: protects the ruined forest.

B. Dark Ranger Commander
- Large enemy archer with cloak and horned mask.
- Color: black, red, silver.
- Attacks: rapid volleys, aimed shot, rain of arrows.
- Theme: enemy archer rival.

C. Fallen Knight
- Heavy armored knight with broken aura.
- Color: steel, red, dark blue.
- Attacks: charge, sword wave, shield bash.
- Theme: old hero turned enemy.

D. Demon Beast
- Large monster with horns and claws.
- Color: dark red, black, orange.
- Attacks: leap, claw swipe, fire burst.
- Theme: late game boss.

7) Wave Progression Idea
Wave 1-3:
- Slimes and goblins.

Wave 4-6:
- Add bats and skeletons.

Wave 7-9:
- Add wolves and elite enemies.

Wave 10:
- Boss fight.

Repeat with stronger stats, more mixed groups, and more elite enemies.

8) Item Design Rules
- Item icons should be small and clear.
- Use one icon per item type.
- Keep rarity color consistent.
- Make equipment look different from consumables.

9) Weapon Item Ideas
- Apprentice Bow.
- Hunter Bow.
- Twin Arrow Bow.
- Silver Longbow.
- Shadow Bow.
- Flame Bow.

10) Armor Item Ideas
- Leather Vest.
- Ranger Cloak.
- Reinforced Armor.
- Wind Robe.
- Shadow Mantle.
- Guardian Mail.

11) Accessory Item Ideas
- Ring of Accuracy.
- Amulet of Speed.
- Charm of Vitality.
- Bracelet of Focus.
- Rune of Power.
- Hunter Token.

12) Consumable Item Ideas
- Small Healing Potion.
- Mana Drink.
- Speed Elixir.
- Arrow Bundle.
- Bomb.
- Skill Scroll.

13) Recommended Visual Theme
- Player: forest ranger archer.
- Enemies: corrupted forest creatures and undead.
- Bosses: guardian, dark ranger, fallen knight, demon beast.
- Items: classic fantasy gear icons.
- Mood: adventure, corruption, magic, and survival.
