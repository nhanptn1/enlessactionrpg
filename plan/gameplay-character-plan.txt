GAMEPLAY AND CHARACTER DESIGN PLAN - Endless Archer Action RPG

1) GAMEPLAY GOAL
- The character auto-attacks.
- The player focuses on movement, upgrades, and survival.
- The game should feel exciting even without manual shooting.
- The player must still be able to fail by bad positioning, weak upgrades, or missed boss patterns.

2) PLAYER CORE LOOP
- Enter wave.
- Enemy swarm appears.
- Archer attacks automatically.
- Player moves to safer position.
- Player collects XP orbs.
- Player levels up and chooses a skill path.
- Player becomes stronger.
- Boss wave appears every 10 waves.
- Repeat with harder waves.

3) WHAT MAKES THE PLAYER EXCITED
- Seeing the build grow every few levels.
- Choosing between Fire, Frost, and Lightning paths.
- Unlocking stronger skill combinations.
- Watching auto-attacks become a powerful screen-clearing build.
- Surviving a wave that looked impossible.

4) HOW THE PLAYER CAN FAIL
- HP reaches zero.
- Too many enemies reach the bottom.
- Player stands in the wrong position.
- Boss telegraph attacks are ignored.
- The build has weak damage or weak crowd control.
- Elite enemies overwhelm the player.

5) PLAYER MOVEMENT DESIGN
- Keep movement simple and readable.
- Player can move left and right at the bottom.
- Movement is the main player control during combat.
- Positioning is the key skill, not aiming.
- Optional dash or dodge can be added later.

6) CHARACTER DESIGN GOAL
- The main character should be easy to read.
- The character must look strong even when idle.
- The bow should always be visible.
- The character should fit the fantasy archer theme.

7) CHARACTER VISUAL STYLE
- Front-facing or slightly 3/4 facing upward.
- Hood or short cloak.
- Leather armor.
- Bow and quiver visible.
- Green, brown, and gold colors.
- Simple silhouette for small-screen readability.

8) CHARACTER ANIMATION SET
- Idle.
- Move left.
- Move right.
- Shoot.
- Hit.
- Death.
- Skill cast.
- Optional dash or dodge.

9) SKILL PATH DESIGN
Fire path:
- High damage.
- Burn effect.
- Explosion damage.
- Good for clearing large groups.

Frost path:
- Slow effect.
- Freeze control.
- Safer gameplay.
- Good against fast waves.

Lightning path:
- Chain attacks.
- Fast burst damage.
- Crit and multi-hit feel.
- Good for high-speed combat.

10) LEVEL-UP DESIGN
- Player gains XP from enemy kills.
- Level-up pauses the game.
- Show 3 upgrade choices.
- Player selects one path or skill.
- Apply the upgrade instantly.
- Return to combat.

11) PROGRESSION MATCH
- Level 1: basic shot.
- Level 3: choose one elemental path.
- Level 5: first branch upgrade.
- Level 8: stronger branch ability.
- Level 12: advanced path upgrade.
- Level 16+: final upgrade tier.

12) ENEMY DESIGN
- Normal enemies should be easy to identify.
- Use different roles for different pressure types.
- Fast enemies create danger.
- Tank enemies force stronger attacks.
- Elite enemies increase wave excitement.

13) WAVE FEEL
- Early waves should feel manageable.
- Mid waves should feel crowded.
- Late waves should feel intense and chaotic.
- Boss waves should feel like major events.

14) FAILURE RULE
- The player should lose because of bad decisions, not because the game is random.
- Good positioning, smart upgrades, and correct path choice should matter.
- Auto-combat should still require real strategy.

15) GODOT IMPLEMENTATION
Create these systems:
- PlayerController.
- AutoAttackSystem.
- SkillTreeManager.
- WaveManager.
- EnemySpawner.
- XPManager.
- LevelUpUI.
- BossManager.

16) MVP DESIGN TARGET
- One archer hero.
- 3 elemental branches.
- 3 to 5 enemy types.
- One boss.
- One upgrade popup.
- One simple movement system.
- One clear fail condition: HP reaches zero.

17) IMPORTANT RULE
- The player should feel powerful through upgrades.
- The game should still punish bad movement and poor build choices.
- The character should be simple, readable, and visually strong from the bottom of the screen.
