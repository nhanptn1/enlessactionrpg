WAVE AND MONSTER COUNT PLAN - Endless Archer Action RPG

1) GOAL
- Make waves feel intense and attractive.
- Use many enemies per wave like other survivor-style games.
- Keep the screen busy, but still readable.
- Match skill upgrades to the bigger enemy count.

2) WAVE SIZE TARGET
- Early waves: 20 to 30 total monsters.
- Mid waves: 40 to 70 total monsters.
- Late waves: 70 to 100 total monsters.
- Boss waves: boss + 10 to 25 support monsters.

3) ACTIVE MONSTER LIMIT
- Do not spawn all monsters at once.
- Keep 10 to 15 monsters active on screen at the same time.
- Spawn the rest in short groups or timed intervals.
- This keeps performance stable and the action easy to read.

4) WAVE PROGRESSION
Wave 1:
- 20 monsters total.
- 5 active at once.

Wave 2:
- 25 monsters total.
- 5 to 6 active at once.

Wave 3:
- 30 monsters total.
- 6 active at once.

Wave 4:
- 35 monsters total.
- 7 active at once.

Wave 5:
- 40 monsters total.
- 8 active at once.

Wave 6:
- 50 monsters total.
- 9 active at once.

Wave 7:
- 60 monsters total.
- 10 active at once.

Wave 8:
- 70 monsters total.
- 12 active at once.

Wave 9:
- 80 monsters total.
- 12 to 15 active at once.

Wave 10:
- Boss wave.
- Boss + 10 to 25 support monsters.

5) LATER WAVE CYCLE
- Waves 11 to 19 repeat the same style.
- Add more enemy HP, speed, and spawn pressure.
- Increase elite enemy chance.
- Add stronger mixed groups.
- Wave 20 becomes a harder boss wave.

6) ENEMY MIX RULE
Use different monster roles in each wave:
- 50% basic enemies.
- 20% fast enemies.
- 15% swarm enemies.
- 10% tank enemies.
- 5% elite or special enemies.

7) SKILL PROGRESSION MATCH
Because waves are large, skills must grow fast enough:
- Level 1: basic shot.
- Level 3: choose Fire, Frost, or Lightning.
- Level 5: first skill upgrade.
- Level 8: stronger crowd-control skill.
- Level 12: advanced branch upgrade.
- Level 16+: final branch version.

8) GAME FEEL
- Many monsters should make the game feel active and exciting.
- The player should always feel pressure from the top of the screen.
- Skill upgrades must help with crowd control and boss damage.
- Large waves should feel fair, not random.

9) GODOT IMPLEMENTATION
Create these systems:
- WaveManager.
- EnemySpawner.
- EnemyPoolManager.
- XPManager.
- SkillTreeManager.
- LevelUpUI.

10) WAVE SYSTEM DATA
Store each wave as data:
- total monsters.
- max active monsters.
- spawn interval.
- enemy type mix.
- boss flag.
- elite chance.

11) PERFORMANCE RULE
- Use object pooling for enemies and projectiles.
- Keep enemy AI simple.
- Keep effects lightweight.
- Avoid spawning all monsters as separate heavy objects at once.

12) MVP TARGET
- Early game: 20 to 40 monsters.
- Mid game: 50 to 80 monsters.
- Late game: 100 monsters maximum.
- Active on screen: 10 to 15 monsters.

13) IMPORTANT DESIGN RULE
- More monsters should increase excitement.
- Skill upgrades should match monster pressure.
- Boss waves should still feel special.
- Keep the screen readable even with many enemies.
