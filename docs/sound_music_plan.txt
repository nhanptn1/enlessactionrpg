SOUND AND MUSIC PLAN - Vertical Endless Archer RPG

1) Audio Goal
- Make the game feel exciting, readable, and responsive.
- Use short sound effects for combat feedback.
- Use music to support the wave-based flow.
- Boss fights should feel louder and more intense.

2) Main Audio Categories
- Music.
- Player actions.
- Enemy actions.
- Boss actions.
- UI sounds.
- Pickup sounds.
- Damage and hit sounds.
- Victory and failure sounds.
- Ambient effects.

3) Music Plan
A. Main Menu Music
- Calm fantasy tone.
- Light mystery or adventure feeling.

B. Gameplay Music
- Fast rhythm.
- Steady pulse.
- Works well for endless waves.

C. Boss Music
- Strong percussion.
- Higher tension.
- More dramatic than normal gameplay.

D. Victory Music
- Short, uplifting melody.
- Play after boss defeat or milestone clear.

E. Game Over Music
- Short sad or tense sting.
- Not too long.

4) Sound Effects for Player
- Bow shot.
- Arrow hit.
- Skill cast.
- Level up.
- Damage taken.
- Heal.
- Dash or dodge.
- UI confirm click.

5) Sound Effects for Enemies
- Spawn sound.
- Move or growl sound.
- Attack sound.
- Hit sound.
- Death sound.
- Elite enemy aura sound.

6) Sound Effects for Bosses
- Boss intro roar or warning cue.
- Heavy attack sound.
- Phase change sound.
- Summon minion sound.
- Special attack warning sound.
- Boss death explosion or collapse sound.

7) UI Sound Design
- Button hover.
- Button click.
- Inventory open.
- Inventory close.
- Skill upgrade select.
- Pause menu open and close.
- Error or locked item click.

8) Pickup Sounds
- Coin pickup.
- XP pickup.
- Item pickup.
- Rare item drop.
- Health or mana pickup.

9) Audio Priority Rules
- Important combat sounds should be louder than background sounds.
- Boss warning sounds should stand out clearly.
- UI sounds should be short and not annoying.
- Music should never hide enemy attack cues.

10) Suggested Audio Mix
- Music: medium volume.
- Player shot: medium-high volume.
- Hit impacts: high clarity.
- UI sounds: low-medium volume.
- Boss attacks: high impact.
- Ambient: low volume.

11) File Naming Example
Music:
- music_menu.ogg
- music_gameplay.ogg
- music_boss.ogg
- music_victory.ogg
- music_gameover.ogg

SFX:
- sfx_arrow_shot_01.wav
- sfx_arrow_hit_01.wav

- sfx_enemy_die_01.wav

- sfx_item_pickup_01.wav

12) Implementation Notes in Godot
- Use AudioStreamPlayer for music.
- Use AudioStreamPlayer2D or AudioStreamPlayer for sound effects.
- Keep music and SFX in separate buses.
- Add volume sliders in settings later.
- Add pitch variation for repeated sounds if needed.

13) Audio Buses Suggestion
- Master.
- Music.
- SFX.
- UI.
- Ambience.

14) MVP Audio Set
- 1 menu music track.
- 1 gameplay music track.
- 1 boss music track.
- 6 player SFX.
- 6 enemy SFX.
- 4 UI SFX.
- 3 pickup SFX.
- 2 boss special SFX.

15) Future Audio Expansion
- Layered boss music phases.
- Ambient forest and ruin sounds.
- Rare item jingle.
- Companion voice effects.
- More enemy variety sounds.
- Dynamic music that changes with wave intensity.
